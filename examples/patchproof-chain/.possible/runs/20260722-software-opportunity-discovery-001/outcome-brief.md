# Outcome brief

## Audience and desired end state

- Operator: a developer who wants to create a useful developer tool but does not yet know which problem is worth solving.
- Current stage: discover one evidence-backed opportunity worth pursuing.
- Finished stage: a traceable recommendation with rejected alternatives, a smallest falsifiable validation experiment, and an independently reviewed `pursue`, `investigate`, or `no-go` decision.

## Current reality

- The workspace contains only the preserved request and the installed Possible skill.
- No existing product, user evidence, audience, distribution advantage, or preferred technical stack was provided.
- The operator has authorized reviewed repo-local skill installation, public-evidence research, and local artifacts for this Discovery stage only.

## Constraints and assumptions

- Prefer opportunities a solo developer can prototype and test locally.
- Do not invent customers, quotes, demand, market size, willingness to pay, revenue, or validation.
- Treat public product documentation, issue trackers, discussions, and reports as demand signals with explicit limits—not proof of willingness to pay.
- No outreach, surveys, accounts, purchases, deployment, publication, spending, or other external mutation is authorized.

## Workstream interfaces

- `opportunity/operator/` records explicit constraints, assumptions, and plausible builder advantages.
- `opportunity/research/` records dated sources and problem evidence.
- `opportunity/landscape/` maps alternatives and underserved gaps without copying competitors.
- `outcome-room/` integrates candidates, scorecard, recommendation, validation experiment, decision receipt, and independent review.

## Acceptance checks

- Produce three to five opportunities traceable to dated public evidence.
- Use one comparable scorecard and preserve uncertainty.
- Select one recommendation and explain rejected alternatives.
- Define the smallest falsifiable next experiment without claiming it has run.
- Write `outcome-room/decision-receipt.json` with exactly one of `pursue`, `investigate`, or `no-go`.
- Independently review evidence traceability, scoring consistency, unsupported claims, and chain readiness.

## External-action gates

All external actions remain unauthorized, including outreach, publishing, surveys, account creation, data purchases, deployment, and spending.

## Unproven claims

Customer urgency, willingness to pay, reachable distribution, product-market fit, market size, and economic viability remain unproven unless direct evidence is recorded.
