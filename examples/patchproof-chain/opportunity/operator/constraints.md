# Builder constraints and advantages

**Recorded:** 2026-07-22  
**Basis:** preserved user request plus current workspace inspection

## Confirmed constraints

- The operator wants a useful developer tool but has not selected a problem or audience.
- The next outcome must be small enough to become a locally verified working web app before launch work begins.
- This stage may research accessible public evidence and create repository-local artifacts.
- Outreach, surveys, account creation, deployment, publishing, spending, and other external mutations are not authorized.

## Builder advantages

No domain-specific unfair advantage was supplied. The following are working hypotheses, not facts:

- The operator can use a coding agent and inspect developer-facing workflows.
- A browser-first, local-only product can be demonstrated without infrastructure or customer data.
- The Outcome Chain can carry a precise discovery receipt into implementation and launch without pretending the opportunity is market-validated.

## Selection implications

Prefer an opportunity with a deterministic core, visible before/after proof, low data sensitivity, and a narrow first user flow. Penalize ideas that require privileged integrations, production credentials, large proprietary datasets, or broad platform emulation.

## Unknowns to resolve later

- Preferred language and framework
- Existing audience or distribution channel
- Appetite for a CLI companion
- Commercial versus open-source intent
- Whether future user research and publication will be authorized
