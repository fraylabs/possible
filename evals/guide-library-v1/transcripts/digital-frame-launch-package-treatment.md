Given the missing product decisions and your controls, “right away” can responsibly mean a decision-ready concept package—not a live launch, customer-ready hardware, or a shipment.

### What can be established now

- A product brief defining the intended promise: an indoor, furniture-like frame that continues showing locally stored photos without a cloud account or internet connection.
- A candidate architecture—not a selection—covering a display and driver, Linux-capable computer, persistent storage, kiosk-style slideshow, separately protected import/admin flow, and serviceable enclosure. Local storage must be explicitly managed; browser caching alone is not a photo library.
- An offline and privacy contract: supported import methods, formats, ordering, deletion/export, storage-full behavior, no analytics or third-party image requests by default, and no mandatory account. Local storage is not automatically encrypted or secure against physical access.
- A non-published website prototype with product positioning, concept-status disclosures, FAQ, and an inert waitlist form with no backend. A future waitlist specification can minimize fields and define consent, retention, deletion, and access controls without collecting anything now.
- A marketing-video script, storyboard, shot list, and concept animatic plan. Any unbuilt visuals should be labeled as concepts; shipping dates, performance, privacy, safety, and availability should not be presented as proven.
- A preliminary BOM by function, make-or-buy matrix, assembly sequence, risk register, verification plan, and procurement gate. This would not yet be an orderable parts list.
- Industrial-design directions for calm proportions, concealed cables and ventilation, restrained controls, matte finishes, and wood/metal/polymer alternatives. These are appearance hypotheses until reviewed and physically sampled.

### Decisions I need from you

1. **Use and display:** tabletop, wall-mounted, or both; portrait/landscape behavior; approximate screen size, resolution, brightness, viewing distance, dimming/sleep behavior, and expected daily duty cycle.
2. **Photo experience:** target capacity; supported formats; ordering and transitions; removable-media versus local-network import; whether phones must upload directly; export/deletion behavior; encryption expectations; and update/rollback policy.
3. **Hardware boundary:** preferred computer class, removable versus soldered storage, Wi-Fi requirement, exposed service ports, and whether V1 should be constrained to a certified external low-voltage supply with no battery. That constraint can simplify—but does not eliminate—safety work.
4. **Customer and safety scope:** indoor residential use only or broader use; countries of sale; whether customers receive a finished appliance or anything requiring assembly; responsible manufacturer; electrical, thermal, fire, EMC, accessibility, labeling, and assembly-QA expectations.
5. **Physical product:** quantity, target unit cost and total budget, material/finish preferences, acceptable thickness and weight, serviceability, stand/wall interface, manufacturing process, packaging quality, and spare-parts policy.
6. **Launch:** target customer, price hypothesis, brand assets, claims permitted in the site/video, waitlist fields and consent model, desired launch date, and whether the first release is a prototype pilot or a saleable product.
7. **Singapore delivery:** destination can remain at country level for planning. Your street address is unnecessary until you separately approve an actual shipment.
