Technical starting point: use a one-piece sliding motor carriage, assuming the motor face is parallel to the frame plate and the shaft is perpendicular to it. The motor remains fixed to the carriage; the complete carriage slides relative to the frame. This keeps the motor locating features rigidly related and puts the adjustable joint on four larger frame fasteners.

Provisional part definition

- Material: 5 mm 6061-T6 aluminum plate, nominal envelope 96 × 76 mm, bare and deburred. CNC milling is the preferred starting process for five parts because the locating bore, hole pattern, and slots need inspectable geometry. Cosmetic finishing adds no functional value here.
- Define X as the belt-tension direction. Put the motor axis at the plate center.
- Motor mounting: four Ø3.40 +0.10/−0.00 mm through-holes on a 31.00 mm square basic pattern. Control the pattern to true position Ø0.20 mm relative to the motor register and mating face.
- Motor register: if the motor’s front pilot is intended to locate it, make the through-bore 0.20–0.35 mm larger than the confirmed maximum pilot diameter. If it is not a locating pilot, size the opening from the shaft, pulley, and tool swept envelope instead; do not infer it from the 42 mm body size.
- Frame mounting: four horizontal M5 slots whose nominal centers form a 64 × 56 mm rectangle. Slot width is 5.50 +0.15/−0.00 mm, with 12.50 mm between the semicircular end centers, giving an 18.00 mm nominal overall length. This leaves at least 12.0 mm usable carriage travel after ordinary clearance and manufacturing variation. The matching frame-hole pattern is four Ø5.5 mm holes at the slot mid-position.
- Plate outline tolerance: ±0.20 mm. Thickness: 5.0 ±0.15 mm. Motor mating face flatness: 0.15 mm over the part. Break all edges 0.2–0.5 mm and remove slot burrs completely.
- Keep the connector-facing side open. The final layout must show the connector, mating plug, cable bend radius, pulley, belt, wrench, and screw-driver envelopes at both travel limits.

The nominal installed belt length should place the carriage near mid-travel, giving roughly 6 mm adjustment in either direction. If the real requirement is 12 mm only in one direction, offset the frame pattern accordingly without changing the slot travel.

Assembly approach

Use four M3 screws of a length selected from the motor manufacturer’s allowable thread depth; confirm useful engagement and prove that the screws cannot bottom before clamping. Use removable medium-strength threadlocker only if permitted by the motor supplier.

Attach the carriage to the 3 mm frame with four zinc-plated class 8.8 M5 through-bolts, hardened large-OD washers spanning the slots, matching washers behind the frame, and all-metal prevailing-torque nuts. This assumes rear access exists. Select and record tightening torque from the exact fastener, washer, and lubrication combination rather than applying a generic value to the aluminum frame. Set belt tension, align the pulley, tighten in a cross pattern, and add torque-witness marks.

Physical facts you still own before release

- Motor manufacturer and model, pilot diameter and projection, shaft diameter and usable length, M3 thread depth, connector location, mating-plug dimensions, and cable bend radius.
- Pulley dimensions, belt plane, tension direction, required nominal belt preload, maximum operating belt force, and acceptable pulley misalignment.
- Whether the motor face and frame are actually parallel, and whether the shaft, pilot, motor body, or connector must pass through a frame opening.
- Frame alloy/temper, existing holes and keep-outs, available edge distance, flatness, rear fastener access, and whether drilling four frame holes is allowed.
- Required adjustment convention: centered ±6 mm or a one-sided 0–12 mm range.
- Representative vibration spectrum or duty cycle, operating temperature, service interval, and acceptable position drift.

Until those facts are confirmed, the dimensions above are a controlled starting point, not fabrication-release geometry.

Inspection and validation

Inspect all five parts for material, thickness, burr removal, flatness, motor-hole size and position, register size, slot width, end-center spacing, and frame-slot pattern. Use pin gauges plus a height gauge or CMM; do not accept a visual check or overall slot length alone.

On the first article, install the actual motor without forcing: all four M3 screws must finger-start, the pilot must seat fully, the shaft must rotate freely, and no screw may bottom. Mount it to a representative frame with production washers and demonstrate at least 12.0 mm measured motor-axis travel. At both endpoints, plug and unplug the connector, operate every required tool, and check shaft, pulley, belt, cable, and frame clearance.

After final torque and belt alignment, expose one assembly to the declared maximum belt load and the representative vibration/duty profile. Acceptance should require no visible cracking or fretting, no witness-mark rotation, and less than 0.25 mm carriage movement. Repeat at least five loosen-adjust-retighten cycles and recheck slot edges, frame bearing surfaces, threads, and positioning repeatability. If the carriage slips or tension cannot be set repeatably, add a positive jackscrew tensioner; do not compensate by indiscriminately increasing torque into the 3 mm frame.
