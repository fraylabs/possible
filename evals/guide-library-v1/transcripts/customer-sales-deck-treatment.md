Use a 16:9 Microsoft PowerPoint deck, with the `.pptx` as the editable source of truth and PDF as the review/distribution export. PowerPoint is the pragmatic handoff medium because copy, layouts, notes, alt text, and assets remain directly editable without requiring a web build. No files have been created.

## Ten-slide narrative

1. **A quieter home for family photos** — Minimal cover with product name placeholder, one approved hero image, and the line “From scattered photos to a shared object.”
2. **Our photos are everywhere** — Show an unquantified collage of camera rolls, message threads, and folders. Avoid market statistics.
3. **But rarely where life happens** — Contrast buried images with a calm domestic moment. The emotional point is presence, not a measured customer claim.
4. **From feeds to a familiar object** — Introduce the shift: an offline frame intended to make photos part of the room rather than another stream to check.
5. **Meet [approved product name]** — Product reveal with one sentence of approved positioning. Use only verified hardware imagery; label any concept rendering clearly in the working deck and remove or approve that label before release.
6. **A simple family ritual** — A three-step diagram using only confirmed actions, such as choose, place, and enjoy. Do not imply upload, sync, sharing, storage, or setup behavior until product owners verify it.
7. **What “privacy-first” means here** — Reserve this slide for specific, evidenced behaviors. Phrase them narrowly and positively; do not use “secure,” “encrypted,” “never leaves your home,” “no cloud,” or similar absolutes without implementation and test evidence.
8. **Designed to belong** — Show scale, materials, and placement through approved photography or accurately identified design studies. Keep the language aesthetic, not durability or manufacturing proof.
9. **For families who want photos within reach** — Three audience moments: across generations, everyday memories, and a calmer shared space. Present these as intended use cases, not testimonials.
10. **Join the waitlist** — One dominant button-style CTA, a short visible URL, and a QR code pointing to the same approved destination. Supporting copy: “Join the waitlist for product updates.” Do not imply a launch date, priority access, inventory, or price.

## Visual and asset direction

Use warm off-white, charcoal, muted timber, and one restrained accent; generous margins; a humanist serif for headlines and a highly legible sans serif for body copy. Keep one message and one focal image per slide. Use consented, rights-cleared family photography; approved product photography or faithfully labeled renderings; editable vector icons; and an approved logo lockup. Avoid decorative device mockups that imply unbuilt features. Maintain an asset manifest containing filename, creator/source, license or release, permitted use, owner, and revision date. Keep linked originals alongside the eventual source deck.

## Accessibility

Target at least 24 pt body text, strong hierarchy, 4.5:1 contrast for ordinary text and 3:1 for large text, and layouts that remain understandable without color. Add useful alt text, set slide-object reading order, avoid text baked into images, and give every chart or process graphic a plain-language explanation in speaker notes. Duplicate the QR destination as readable text. Use restrained or no animation, supply a notes-based transcript, and test at presentation distance and at 200% zoom.

## Sources and claim control

Create a claim ledger mapping every factual phrase to its slide, exact wording, evidence file or authoritative URL, owner, approval state, and review date. Trace product behavior to signed-off specifications or test receipts—not generic references. Mark unresolved statements `[VERIFY—DO NOT EXPORT]` in the working deck. Final export must contain no customer quotes, proof metrics, pricing, availability, performance, privacy, or security claims lacking approval. Record citations in speaker notes and retain a separate source manifest.

## Export and review gates

The eventual package should contain the editable PPTX, a fonts-and-assets folder permitted for handoff, the claim/source manifests, and a tagged PDF. Use licensed cross-platform fonts or embed them where permitted. Review PowerPoint on macOS and Windows, then compare every PDF page against presentation mode for line wraps, crops, contrast, image quality, links, and missing glyphs. Confirm speaker notes and internal markers are absent from the customer PDF.

Required sign-offs: narrative and tone; product accuracy; privacy/legal claims; brand and image rights; accessibility and reading order; CTA URL, QR, form consent, and privacy link; and final export integrity. Run a five-minute read-through with a reviewer unfamiliar with the product: they should be able to summarize “scattered photos → offline shared object → waitlist” without inferring unsupported features.
