## What this makes possible

A revision-matched drawing that carries the critical feature controls, notes, and inspection expectations that solid geometry alone may not communicate safely.

## A common approach

Attach a drawing when critical tolerances, threads, finishes, datums, or inspection requirements cannot be safely inferred from the 3D model and quote form.

## Use this when

Use this practice when specific features have functional acceptance limits and the provider accepts supporting drawings with the model.

## Limits and alternatives

If every required manufacturing and inspection attribute is already represented and confirmed through another accepted contract, a separate drawing may be unnecessary. Use [Tolerance contract](/wiki/tolerance-contract) to decide which features actually need drawing-level controls.

## Important decisions

This is part of the [Custom manufactured parts](/wiki/custom-manufactured-parts) handoff package. [Tolerance contract](/wiki/tolerance-contract) defines the feature tolerances and datum strategy that the drawing communicates. [STEP solid exchange](/wiki/step-solid-exchange) remains the neutral solid model commonly paired with the drawing.

## How to verify

Confirm that drawing revision, units, model revision, and inspection expectations all agree.
