## What this makes possible

Manufacturing knowledge covers outcomes that end in fabricated parts. It keeps a traceable chain from native design intent through process-specific artifacts, provider handoffs, fabricated parts, and inspection evidence.

## A common approach

Start here when the requested outcome includes one or more physical custom parts. Keep the native design, exported artifacts, approvals, and inspection evidence aligned, then narrow into [Custom manufactured parts](/wiki/custom-manufactured-parts) when a specific part must be quoted or fabricated.

## Use this when

Use this branch when the outcome includes one or more physical custom parts and a manufacturing provider or local fabrication process will consume exported artifacts.

## Consider another route when

If the outcome is wholly digital and never produces a physical component, use [Web](/wiki/web) instead.

## Important decisions

Web interfaces often initiate or monitor custom-part workflows, so Manufacturing and [Web](/wiki/web) can meet in the same project even when the fabricated part remains the core outcome.
